The Clockwork Owl

In a clocktower at the edge of the village lived a clockwork owl, wound each dusk by the old bell-keeper to chase away the mice that gnawed at the bell ropes. The owl performed its task with perfect precision, swiveling its brass head exactly ninety degrees every hour, never more, never less.

One night the bell-keeper died, and no one came to wind the owl again. Its gears slowed, its eyes dimmed, but it kept turning its head anyway, by memory alone, long after the springs had lost their strength.

A real owl passing by asked why it bothered, unwound as it was. The clockwork owl said it did not know how to be anything but watchful.

The real owl was so moved by this stubborn devotion that she began roosting in the tower herself, guarding the bells the old owl no longer could.
